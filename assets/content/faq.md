## Do you see clients in-person?

At this time, all of my sessions are conducted virtually (telehealth).

Research shows that virtual therapy is just as effective as in-person therapy. This holds specifically for anxiety disorders, eating disorders, and OCD.

## Do you work with both kids and adults?

Yes! I work with kids (ages 8+), teens, parents, and adults.

## What clients do you work with?

Most of my clients are dealing with anxiety, OCD, eating disorders, and/or perinatal mood disorders (pregnancy and postpartum depression, OCD, and anxiety).

My expertise includes OCD, generalized anxiety, social anxiety, health anxiety, separation anxiety, panic disorder, phobias, perfectionism, depression, perinatal and postpartum OCD, anxiety, and depression, anorexia nervosa, atypical anorexia, bulimia nervosa, ARFID, and binge eating disorder.

As a queer therapist myself, I welcome LGBTQIA+ clients and families with care that is identity-affirming and grounded in real understanding.

## Do you use ERP when treating OCD?

Yes, I am trained in exposure and response prevention therapy (ERP), the evidence-based, first-line treatment for OCD in both kids and adults. I always consider the individual when deciding whether to utilize ERP, and may recommend other approaches depending on presentation or co-occurring conditions.

## How long are therapy sessions and how often do we meet?

After intake, therapy sessions are 45 minutes in length unless otherwise specified. Intake sessions are 60 minutes in length.

Therapy is structured with your success in mind. Therefore, clients are asked to commit to at least weekly therapy initially. Occasionally, I may recommend stepping frequency up or down depending on the circumstances.

## Do you accept insurance?

I am an out-of-network provider, so I do not accept insurance directly. I do provide monthly superbills that can be submitted to your insurance for out-of-network reimbursement. Credit cards, HSA, and FSA cards are accepted for payment.

## Why don’t you take insurance directly?

Insurance-based mental health care is built around seeing as many clients as possible in the shortest time, and decisions are reviewed by people who have not met you.

This practice is built around the opposite:

- Treatment guided by clinical judgment, not insurance approval
- Care coordination with schools, dieticians, and doctors, as needed 
- Confidential, individualized, high-quality care

If you have health insurance, you may have more out-of-network benefits for mental health than you realize. I recommend prospective clients call the number on the back of their insurance card and ask the following questions:

* Does my plan include “out-of-network” coverage for mental health counseling?
* What is my co-insurance? (What percentage does the plan cover?)
* What is my out-of-network mental health deductible?
* Is there a limit on the number of sessions my plan will cover per year?
* How do I submit for reimbursement?
