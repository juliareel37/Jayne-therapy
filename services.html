Individual Therapy

Individual therapy is a private, consistent space to explore your emotions, relationships, patterns, stressors, and goals. Sessions may include reflection, emotional processing, practical tools, and deeper work around the experiences that shaped how you relate to yourself and others.

Jayne's approach is collaborative and personalized. Therapy may help you better understand anxiety, build boundaries, process grief, reduce self-criticism, navigate change, and respond to your life with more clarity and self-trust.

You may not be in crisis, but something still feels unsustainable. You may be functioning, achieving, caregiving, or showing up for everyone else while feeling increasingly disconnected from yourself.

If any of this feels familiar, therapy can offer a place to understand the pattern rather than keep pushing through it.

Individual therapy may be a fit if you:

  • Overthink decisions or replay conversations
  • Feel responsible for other people's emotions
  • Struggle to set boundaries without guilt
  • Avoid conflict, then feel resentful or unseen
  • Feel burned out from being dependable
  • Are hard on yourself even when doing your best
  • Feel anxious, restless, or emotionally exhausted
  • Are navigating a breakup, move, career change, loss, or identity shift
  • Want to understand yourself more deeply, not just "cope better"

Areas of Focus

Anxiety & Overthinking

Anxiety can show up as racing thoughts, perfectionism, physical tension, trouble sleeping, avoidance, or the feeling that something is always about to go wrong. Therapy can help you understand what drives your anxiety and build steadier ways to respond.

Burnout & Chronic Stress

Burnout often affects people who are used to pushing through, staying responsible, and being dependable. Therapy can help you examine the pressure you are carrying, the beliefs that keep you overextending, and the boundaries needed to move at a more sustainable pace.

Relationship Patterns & Boundaries

Relationships can bring old patterns to the surface: people-pleasing, conflict avoidance, overexplaining, withdrawing, or feeling responsible for others' emotions. Therapy can help you understand these dynamics and practice more honest, grounded ways of relating.

Grief & Loss

Grief is not always neat, visible, or easy to explain. Jayne supports clients through death, estrangement, breakups, ambiguous loss, anticipatory grief, and life changes that involve letting go of something meaningful.

Life Transitions

Transitions can be disorienting even when they are chosen or positive. Therapy can support you through moves, career changes, breakups, graduation, family shifts, identity changes, and seasons where life no longer feels familiar.

Self-Worth & Identity

Self-worth struggles may show up as shame, comparison, perfectionism, self-doubt, or difficulty knowing what you want. Therapy can help you build a more compassionate relationship with yourself and make choices that feel more aligned with your values.

How Therapy Is Tailored

Therapy is shaped around your goals, history, pace, and needs. Jayne draws from several evidence-informed approaches rather than using a single method for every client.

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy

CBT offers a structured way to understand the links between thoughts, emotions, beliefs, and behavior.

Acceptance and Commitment Therapy

ACT supports values-based action, emotional flexibility, and a different relationship to difficult internal experiences.

Attachment-Based Therapy

Attachment-based work explores how relationship experiences shape expectations for trust, closeness, conflict, and care.

Psychodynamic Insight

This approach makes room for family-of-origin dynamics, unconscious expectations, and recurring relational themes.

Mindfulness-Informed Therapy

Mindfulness-informed work can support greater awareness of emotions, thoughts, and body cues as they arise.

Trauma-Informed Care

Jayne works with attention to emotional safety, pacing, collaboration, choice, and respect for each client's lived experience.

What Sessions Look Like

Therapy with Jayne is reflective, collaborative, and connected to real life. Sessions often include three overlapping parts:

01

Understanding the pattern

You and Jayne explore what is happening now, where certain patterns may have started, and how they affect your emotions, relationships, and choices.

02

Building practical tools

Depending on your needs, therapy may include tools for grounding, boundaries, communication, emotional regulation, decision-making, and responding differently to anxiety or conflict.

03

Creating meaningful change

Over time, therapy can help you move from automatic reactions toward choices that feel more honest, steady, and aligned with your needs.
